The Power of Compounding: An Automated Sports Analysis System

Ever heard the saying about choosing between a penny that doubles every day for a month versus $1 million upfront? By day 30, that penny grows to $10,737,418.24—far exceeding the million dollars. Now, imagine applying this concept in real life.

This case study explores how the principles of compounding, money management, automation, and data-driven insights can be applied to low-risk soccer wagers.

This isn't just about wagering or making $10,737,418.24—it's about leveraging data and smart automation to achieve scalable, consistent success in financial markets.

Team: Me (Solo Project) Duration: 2 Months

The Problems

  • Inconsistent Results: Poor predictions based on subjective intuition
  • Financial Risks: No standardized strategies to mitigate losses
  • Lack of Automation: Manual sports analysis is slow and error-prone
  • Ethical Concerns: Gambling addiction and unregulated influences

Goals

  • Automate: End-to-end automation for sport data analysis and wagering
  • Enable Sustainable Growth: Apply compounding strategies for growth
  • Ensure Scalability: Adapt system to other sports or financial markets
  • Promote Ethics: Address gambling addiction and regulatory concerns

System Overview

Stage 1: Data Scrapping

  • Scraping Odds for Upcoming Games:
    • Focus is on low odds with high-probability
    • Odds Range: (1.05 ~ 95.2%, 1.10 ~ 90.9%)
    • Odds Type: Over/Under - corners, goals, etc.
    • Data Source: OddsPortal, Bet365, etc.
  • System scrapes historical game data
    • Team performance: H2H records, recent form
    • Player stats: Shots, substitutions, injuries
    • Data Source: ESPN, FootyStats, etc.
  • Data cleaning or preprocessing

Stage 2: AI-Driven Analysis

  • AI is trained on data for predictions
  • AI tools:
    • XGBoost: Predicts event probabilities
    • Random Forest: Validates and flags outlies
    • TensorFlow: Adjusts predictions with live data
  • Testing/Validations
    • 3 accounts with different XGBoost setups
    • 1 account for manual bet placement
    • 1 control account (no bets placed)
  • Fall back logic to overide AI decisions

Stage 3: Wagering

  • AI matches predictions to scrapped odds
  • Automated bet placement
    • APIs for integration with betting platforms
    • Selenium automates platforms without APIs
  • Money Management:
    • Start with small bets (e.g., $2)
    • Apply Kelly Criterion to set optimal bet size
    • Daily Cap: limit to 5 bets/day, 1 bet/match
    • Compound winnings for exponential returns
  • Stop loss for safety measures

The Good, Bad, & Ugly

The Good

  • AI models can be refined for greater accuracy
  • Incoporation of real-time data and NLP
  • Modular design for new feature integration

The Bad

  • Poor data quality impacts predictions
  • Automation complications for Non-API sites
  • Risk of overfitting AI models to specific scenarios

The Ugly

  • Potential risk of encouraging gambling addiction
  • Varying regional laws can affect system use
  • Risks of down servers, API changes, or code bugs

Ethical Disclaimer

This project is for educational purposes only and does not promote gambling. Users of the system must comply with local laws and practice gamble responsibly, understanding the risks involved.

Final Thoughts

This project demonstrates data-driven strategies in financial markets, focusing on automation and risk management to achieve long-term success, with the potential to reach $10,737,418.24-Ideally.

Compatibility Assessment of Biosensor with Foam Dressings for Infection Detection

This project evaluates the compatibility of a biosensor with commercially available foam dressings to determine which-materials and thicknesses-work best for infection detection in both high and low exudate wounds.

Biosensor Overview: Early detection of bacterial infection in skin wounds is crucial to prevent chronic issues. Current methods for detecting low bacterial concentrations (<1.0 × 105 CFU/cm2) are often slow, invasive, or expensive. Dr. Song’s group developed a colorimetric nanofibrous biosensor that monitors bacterial levels in real-time. Using Hemicyanine (HCy) dye, it changes color in response to lipase—an enzyme secreted by bacteria to break down fats and minerals for consumption—enabling early detection before infection begins.

Role: Research Assistant Duration: 5 Months

Acknowledgments: Dr. Song Liu, P.Eng, Farinaz Jonidi Shariatzadeh, PhD

Biosensor Key Insights

  • Electrospun from polymers + HCy solution
  • Color: Yellow to green upon lipase reaction
  • Setup: Attached on dressings, secured with film
  • Bacterias Tested: E. coli, MRSA, P. aeruginosa

Biosensor and Foam Dressing Tests

  • Foam dressing absorption test
  • Biosensor dye penetration test
  • Testing with artificial wound fluid (AWF)
  • Bacteria testing on Agar - with/without AWF
  • Bacteria testing on porcine skin (Ex vivo)
  • Antibacterial solution test (Vashe)

Main Findings

  • Dressings with fluid-resistant top layers impact biosensor performance
  • Exudate volume affects biosensor response time
  • 7 foam dressings tested; most compatible:
    • Cura-Foam, HydroFera, Qwick, Aqucacel, and Optifoam Basic

Interactive Exhibit Project (Capstone)

For my capstone project, I collaborated with the Royal Aviation Museum of Western Canada (RAMWC) to design and prototype an interactive Exhibit Project. Targeted at students (grades 7 and below) and museum visitors, the exhibit had two main goals:

  • Explain aviation fuel complcopyrighties and the transition to sustainable options.
  • Inspire interest in STEM careers to address sustainable aviation challenges.
Aviation contributes significantly to carbon emissions, and transitioning to sustainable fuels poses challenges such as infrastructure limitations, aircraft weight, energy density, and storage. The exhibit was designed to present these intricacies in a fun, accessible way to inspire future innovators.

Role: Instrumentation and CAD Drafting Duration: 8 Months

Acknowledgments: RAMWC, Camryn McMillan (Team member)

Project Specifications

  • Deliver three learning outcomes:
    • History of aviation fuel (past to present)
    • Energy density comparisons of different fuel options
    • Carbon emissions comparisons of different fuel options
  • Adhere to constraints like height, space, durability, cost, power, etc.
  • Accessible for all ages and abilities, including wheelchair users

Proposed Solution

  • A three-part exhibit featuring:
    • Timeline Wall
    • Carbon Emission Station
    • Energy Density Station
  • Achieved all learning outcomes with an engaging and interactive design
  • Achieved an 85% satisfactory rating during exhibit viewing
